# Apples To Apples

Guess what your friends will find accurate, amusing, or just plain ridiculous!

## Preparation

These steps should be done before the game is introduced to new players.

* Remove all the "My ..." cards from the red deck.

## Setup & Explanation

Note that I find the game more fun if the judge hams up their narration of the cards a little.
Not everyone agrees, but then not everyone is correct.

1. Insert a green card near the top of the red deck.
1. Deal 7 cards from the red deck to each player.
1. Whoever receives the green card is the judge, and does the following instructions.
1. Place the green card face-up in the middle of the table and state "From your red cards, choose one that I will think best matches this card. I might consider accuracy, humour, satire or any other criteria. Place it face-down in front of you, then draw back up to 7 cards.".
1. Draw back up to 7 red cards, while everyone is choosing what card they should play.
1. Once everyone has played a red card, gather them up, shuffle them, and read them aloud.
1. Choose one of the cards, with a brief explanation of why you chose it. State "Whoever played that card gets the green card, just keep it in front of you as score.".
1. Explain that the judge role moves clockwise around the table, indicate who the next judge is and clarify that judges don't play red cards. Let them know to draw a green card and repeat the process.

An easy way for a club organiser to introduce the game is to judge the first round, then supervise the second round with a different judge.
This allows you to explain the rules and demonstrate how to play without having to commit to the full game.

## Winning

Play for 12 rounds, and then until everyone has judged the same number of rounds.
The person with the most green cards in front of them has won.

## That wasn't personal enough...

* Explain that if a card has "My ..." on it, it is from the judge's perspective, as they cannot know who played it.
* Shuffle the "My ..." cards into the red deck.
